6 Days Workout Plans

Choose your fitness level and start your transformation

Beginner Level: 6-Day Plan

For those who are new to fitness or returning after a long break. Focus on learning proper form and building baseline strength.

Day 1: Bt, Pt, Dt, Pf
Day 2: W, Bi, M, Bi
Day 3: Di, Di, Tr, Lt
Day 4: Lt, Gi, Cr, Bs
Day 5: Rw, Rt, Lr, Pf
Day 6: Bs, Pu, Sh, Jj

Day 1: Full Body

Bodyweight Squats: 3 sets of 12 reps
Push-ups (or Knee Push-ups): 3 sets of 8-10 reps
Dumbbell Rows: 3 sets of 10 reps each arm
Plank: 3 sets of 20-30 seconds

Day 2: Light Cardio & Core

Walking/Light Jogging: 20 minutes
Bicycle Crunches: 3 sets of 10 reps each side
Mountain Climbers: 3 sets of 30 seconds
Bird Dogs: 3 sets of 8 reps each side

Day 3: Upper Body Focus

Dumbbell Shoulder Press: 3 sets of 10 reps
Dumbbell Chest Press: 3 sets of 10 reps
Tricep Dips (using chair): 3 sets of 8 reps
Lateral Raises: 3 sets of 10 reps

Day 4: Lower Body Focus

Lunges: 3 sets of 10 reps each leg
Glute Bridges: 3 sets of 12 reps
Calf Raises: 3 sets of 15 reps
Bodyweight Squats: 3 sets of 12 reps

Day 5: Cardio & Core

Brisk Walking/Jogging: 25 minutes
Russian Twists: 3 sets of 10 reps each side
Leg Raises: 3 sets of 10 reps
Plank: 3 sets of 30 seconds

Day 6: Full Body

Bodyweight Squats: 3 sets of 15 reps
Push-ups: 3 sets of 10 reps
Superman Hold: 3 sets of 20 seconds
Jumping Jacks: 3 sets of 30 seconds

Rest on Day 7. Start with light weights and focus on form. Increase weight or reps gradually as you get stronger.

Intermediate Level: 6-Day Split

For those with 6+ months of consistent training. This plan increases intensity and introduces more targeted muscle group training.

Day 1: BP, IP, CF, TP, OE
Day 2: PU, BR, SR, BC, HC
Day 3: BS, RD, LP, LC, HL, CC
Day 4: OP, LR, FP, UR, SH, PL, RT
Day 5: HIIT Cardio
Day 6: DL, PU, PU, WP

Day 1: Chest & Triceps

Barbell Bench Press: 4 sets of 8-10 reps
Incline Dumbbell Press: 3 sets of 10 reps
Chest Flyes: 3 sets of 12 reps
Tricep Pushdowns: 3 sets of 12 reps
Overhead Tricep Extensions: 3 sets of 12 reps

Day 2: Back & Biceps

Pull-ups or Lat Pulldowns: 4 sets of 8-10 reps
Bent Over Rows: 3 sets of 10 reps
Seated Cable Rows: 3 sets of 12 reps
Barbell Bicep Curls: 3 sets of 10 reps
Hammer Curls: 3 sets of 12 reps

Day 3: Legs & Core

Barbell Squats: 4 sets of 10 reps
Romanian Deadlifts: 3 sets of 10 reps
Leg Press: 3 sets of 12 reps
Leg Curls: 3 sets of 12 reps
Hanging Leg Raises: 3 sets of 12 reps
Cable Crunches: 3 sets of 15 reps

Day 4: Shoulders & Abs

Overhead Press: 4 sets of 8-10 reps
Lateral Raises: 3 sets of 12 reps
Face Pulls: 3 sets of 15 reps
Upright Rows: 3 sets of 10 reps
Shrugs: 3 sets of 12 reps
Plank: 3 sets of 45-60 seconds
Russian Twists: 3 sets of 15 reps each side

Day 5: HIIT Cardio

Warm-up: 5 minutes light cardio
Interval Training: 20 minutes (30 seconds high intensity, 30 seconds rest)
Exercises include: Burpees, Mountain Climbers, Jump Squats, High Knees
Cool down: 5 minutes easy cardio + stretching

Day 6: Full Body & Weak Points

Deadlifts: 4 sets of 8 reps
Push-ups: 3 sets of 15-20 reps
Pull-ups: 3 sets of 8-10 reps
Focus on weak points: 2-3 targeted exercises

Rest on Day 7. Perform 5-10 minutes of warm-up before each workout. Adjust weights to reach near failure on the last 1-2 reps of each set.

Advanced Level: 6-Day Split

For experienced lifters with 2+ years of training. This high-volume program focuses on hypertrophy with strength elements.

Day 1: BP, IP, WD, CF, CG, TP, OE
Day 2: DL, WPU, BR, TR, BC, IC, CC
Day 3: BS, FS, RD, LP, WL, SCR, WHL, AWR
Day 4: MP, PP, LR, FP, UR, SH, CC, DBS
Day 5: IDP, CR, DP, PU, LR, FP, TP, EZC
Day 6: HS, BSS, LE, LC, SCR, COND

Day 1: Chest & Triceps (Heavy)

Bench Press: 5 sets of 5 reps
Incline Bench Press: 4 sets of 8 reps
Weighted Dips: 3 sets of 8-10 reps
Cable Flyes: 3 sets of 12 reps
Close-Grip Bench: 4 sets of 8 reps
Tricep Pushdowns: 3 sets of 12 reps
Overhead Extensions: 3 sets of 12 reps

Day 2: Back & Biceps (Heavy)

Deadlifts: 5 sets of 5 reps
Weighted Pull-ups: 4 sets of 6-8 reps
Bent-Over Rows: 4 sets of 8 reps
T-Bar Rows: 3 sets of 10 reps
Barbell Curls: 4 sets of 8 reps
Incline Dumbbell Curls: 3 sets of 10 reps
Concentration Curls: 3 sets of 12 reps

Day 3: Legs (Heavy) & Core

Back Squats: 5 sets of 5 reps
Front Squats: 4 sets of 8 reps
Romanian Deadlifts: 4 sets of 8 reps
Leg Press: 3 sets of 10 reps
Walking Lunges: 3 sets of 12 steps each leg
Standing Calf Raises: 4 sets of 15 reps
Weighted Hanging Leg Raises: 4 sets of 12 reps
Ab Wheel Rollouts: 3 sets of 12 reps

Day 4: Shoulders & Abs (Heavy)

Military Press: 5 sets of 5 reps
Push Press: 4 sets of 6 reps
Lateral Raises: 4 sets of 12 reps
Face Pulls: 3 sets of 15 reps
Upright Rows: 3 sets of 10 reps
Shrugs: 4 sets of 12 reps
Cable Crunches: 4 sets of 15 reps
Decline Bench Sit-ups: 3 sets of 15 reps

Day 5: Upper Body Volume

Incline Dumbbell Press: 4 sets of 10 reps
Cable Rows: 4 sets of 10 reps
Dips: 3 sets of 12 reps
Pull-ups: 3 sets of 12 reps
Lateral Raises: 3 sets of 15 reps
Face Pulls: 3 sets of 15 reps
Tricep Pushdowns: 3 sets of 15 reps
EZ Bar Curls: 3 sets of 15 reps

Day 6: Lower Body Volume & Conditioning

Hack Squats: 4 sets of 12 reps
Bulgarian Split Squats: 3 sets of 10 reps each leg
Leg Extensions: 3 sets of 15 reps
Leg Curls: 3 sets of 15 reps
Seated Calf Raises: 4 sets of 20 reps
Conditioning Circuit: 3 rounds of 45 seconds work/15 seconds rest
Circuit includes: Battle Ropes, Box Jumps, Kettlebell Swings, Sled Push

Rest on Day 7. Incorporate proper warm-up sets before your working sets. For heavy days, use 80-90% of your 1RM. Track your progress and aim to increase weight or reps each week.

Always consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new fitness regimen.
